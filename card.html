Armadillo

Armadillo shells have traditionally been used to make the back of the charango, an Andean lute instrument.

  • Name: The name armadillo came from the Spanish terms meaning “little armored one.”
  • Eatable: You can eat armadillo.
  • Mascot: The three-banded armadillo was the mascot of the 2014 World Cup tournament in Brazil.
  • Procreation: Nine-banded armadillos always give birth to identical quadruplets.

Armadillos are small to medium-sized mammals. They are prolific diggers. Many species use their sharp claws to dig for food, such as grubs, and to dig dens. The diet of the armadillo consists mainly of insects, grubs, and other invertebrates.
